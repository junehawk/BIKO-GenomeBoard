Pipeline Description

The GenomeBoard Consilium pipeline performs multi-agent variant interpretation integrating ACMG/AMP 2015 classification criteria with Korean population-specific frequency data from the Korea Reference Genome Database (KRGDB v3.0). Each variant undergoes parallel evaluation by three AI-assisted consilium agents:

  • Clinical Agent — queries ClinVar for significance calls, associated conditions, and review star ratings
  • Korean Population Agent — compares allele frequencies between KRGDB and gnomAD (EAS/global), flags variants with ≥2x enrichment in Korean cohorts
  • Pharmacogenomics Agent — cross-references CPIC and PharmGKB guidelines with Korean-specific prevalence adjustments

Final classification follows ACMG/AMP 2015 criteria with ClinGen SVI updates. Korean enrichment ratio is calculated as KRGDB frequency / gnomAD global frequency.

Limitations
  • This report is generated for research purposes only and has not been validated in a CLIA-certified or CAP-accredited laboratory.
  • KRGDB frequency data reflects the Korean genome cohort; individual ancestry may vary.
  • ClinVar entries reflect community-submitted evidence and may contain conflicting interpretations.
  • PGx phenotype predictions are probabilistic; functional testing (e.g., HLA typing for HLA-B*5801) is required for confirmatory clinical use.
  • Variants of uncertain significance (VUS) may be reclassified as new evidence accrues; periodic re-analysis is recommended.
  • This report does not constitute medical advice. Clinical decisions must involve a board-certified clinical geneticist or genetic counselor.

CPIC Evidence Levels for Pharmacogenomics (PGx)

The Clinical Pharmacogenetics Implementation Consortium (CPIC) assigns evidence levels to gene-drug pairs to guide clinical implementation of pharmacogenomic test results.

LevelClassificationDescription
Level A Strong evidence Prescribing action recommended. Strong evidence linking the gene-drug pair to a clinical outcome, with a published CPIC guideline providing specific dosing or drug selection recommendations.
Level B Moderate evidence Prescribing action may be recommended. Moderate evidence supports changing pharmacotherapy based on genetic information, but a full CPIC guideline may not yet be available.
Level C Optional evidence Clinical action is optional. Evidence supports a gene-drug association, but the clinical impact of adjusting therapy is unclear or the magnitude of effect is small.
Level D Informational only No prescribing action recommended. Evidence is insufficient to support changing clinical practice based on this gene-drug pair at this time.

Reference: Relling MV, Klein TE. CPIC: Clinical Pharmacogenetics Implementation Consortium of the Pharmacogenomics Research Network. Clin Pharmacol Ther. 2011;89(3):464-467. PMID: 21270786. CPIC guidelines and gene-drug pair assignments are updated regularly at cpicpgx.org.
