Clinical Significance
Treatment Strategies
When Li-Fraumeni syndrome is confirmed, a whole-body cancer surveillance protocol (Toronto protocol) is recommended, including annual whole-body MRI, breast MRI (for females), brain MRI, and abdominal ultrasound. Radiation exposure should be minimized, as radiation therapy carries a risk of secondary tumor induction and should be avoided when possible. Surgical resection is the primary treatment for cancer occurrence; responsiveness to platinum-based chemotherapy and immunotherapy (e.g., pembrolizumab) is evaluated individually. Cascade genetic testing of family members is essential, and preimplantation genetic testing (PGT) may be considered for germline variant carriers.

The population frequency of pathogenic TP53 germline variants is estimated at approximately 1/5,000 to 1/20,000. For cystic fibrosis, CFTR modulator therapy (ivacaftor, lumacaftor/ivacaftor, elexacaftor/tezacaftor/ivacaftor) is applied depending on the variant type. Cystic fibrosis occurs at a frequency of approximately 1/2,500 in Caucasians, making it the most common autosomal recessive disease in that population, with a carrier frequency of approximately 1/25. Clinical Significance
Treatment Strategies
When a CYP2C19 poor metabolizer is identified, antiplatelet therapy is switched from clopidogrel to prasugrel or ticagrelor; this is recommended at Level A evidence by CPIC and DPWG guidelines. When using proton pump inhibitors (PPIs), drug concentrations are elevated in PMs, so dose reduction of omeprazole or substitution with an alternative PPI (rabeprazole) is considered. When prescribing antidepressants (escitalopram, sertraline), the possibility of elevated blood concentrations in PMs warrants starting at a low dose and performing therapeutic drug monitoring (TDM). It is advisable to register pharmacogenomic results in the electronic medical record (EMR) so that automatic alerts are generated during future prescriptions.

The CYP2C19 *2 allele frequency in Koreans is approximately 25–29%, significantly higher than in Western populations (approximately 12–15%). Pipeline Description

The GenomeBoard Consilium pipeline performs multi-agent variant interpretation integrating ACMG/AMP 2015 classification criteria with Korean population-specific frequency data from the Korea Reference Genome Database (KRGDB v3.0). Each variant undergoes parallel evaluation by three AI-assisted consilium agents:

  • Clinical Agent — queries ClinVar for significance calls, associated conditions, and review star ratings
  • Korean Population Agent — compares allele frequencies between KRGDB and gnomAD (EAS/global), flags variants with ≥2x enrichment in Korean cohorts
  • Pharmacogenomics Agent — cross-references CPIC and PharmGKB guidelines with Korean-specific prevalence adjustments

Final classification follows ACMG/AMP 2015 criteria with ClinGen SVI updates. Korean enrichment ratio is calculated as KRGDB frequency / gnomAD global frequency.
