# Cutaneous Melanoma — Treatment Overview

> Curated summary from CIViC / publicly-available guideline sources. Not official NCCN. For educational/decision-support context only.

## Targeted Therapy
- **BRAF V600E/K**: Dabrafenib + trametinib, encorafenib + binimetinib, or vemurafenib + cobimetinib (Evidence: A)
- **BRAF V600 adjuvant (stage III resected)**: Dabrafenib + trametinib (Evidence: A)
- **NRAS Q61**: MEK inhibitor (binimetinib) — modest benefit, trial preferred (Evidence: B)
- **KIT exon 11/13 (acral/mucosal)**: Imatinib (Evidence: B)
- **NTRK fusion**: Larotrectinib or entrectinib (Evidence: A)

## Immunotherapy
- **Metastatic first-line**: Nivolumab + ipilimumab, or nivolumab + relatlimab, or pembrolizumab/nivolumab monotherapy (Evidence: A)
- **Adjuvant (stage IIB–IV resected)**: Pembrolizumab or nivolumab (Evidence: A)
- **Neoadjuvant (stage III)**: Pembrolizumab (SWOG S1801) (Evidence: A)
- **TIL therapy (post-ICI failure)**: Lifileucel (Evidence: B)

## Chemotherapy
- Historically: dacarbazine, temozolomide, carboplatin + paclitaxel
- Limited role in modern practice; reserved for ICI/targeted failure
