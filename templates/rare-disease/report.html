Pipeline Description

The BIKO GenomeBoard Rare Disease pipeline performs germline variant interpretation integrating ACMG/AMP 2015 classification criteria with HPO phenotype matching, OMIM gene-disease associations, ClinGen gene-disease validity curation, and Korean population-specific frequency data from KOVA v7 (Korean Variant Archive, ~43M variants).

  • HPO Phenotype Matching — resolves patient HPO terms to associated gene lists; HPO score = number of patient phenotypes linked to each candidate gene
  • Candidate Ranking — variants sorted by classification tier (Pathogenic first) then HPO score (descending)
  • OMIM Integration — gene-disease associations and inheritance patterns from OMIM genemap2 database
  • ClinGen Gene Validity — curated gene-disease validity classifications (Definitive → Refuted)
  • Korean Population Frequency — compares KOVA v7 against gnomAD (EAS/global); KOVA frequency drives BA1/BS1/PM2
  • In Silico Predictions — REVEL, CADD, AlphaMissense, SpliceAI scores from VEP annotations; PP3/BP4 evidence generated per ClinGen SVI 2022 recommendations (REVEL primary for missense, SpliceAI for splice variants)
  • ACMG Evidence Collection — automated PVS1 (null variant + LOF intolerance), PM1 (functional domain), PM4 (in-frame indel), PP2 (constrained gene), BP7 (synonymous) in addition to ClinVar and frequency-based codes
  • Pharmacogenomics — cross-references CPIC and PharmGKB guidelines with Korean-specific prevalence adjustments

Limitations
  • This report is generated for research purposes only and has not been validated in a CLIA-certified or CAP-accredited laboratory.
  • HPO gene associations are derived from the Monarch Initiative and JAX HPO database; completeness varies by phenotype.
  • OMIM data reflects static curated entries; dynamic API lookup requires an OMIM API key.
  • ClinGen validity classifications reflect the state of curation at time of report generation.
  • KOVA frequency data reflects the Korean genome cohort; individual ancestry may vary.
  • Variants of uncertain significance (VUS) may be reclassified as new evidence accrues; periodic re-analysis is recommended.
  • This report does not constitute medical advice. Clinical decisions must involve a board-certified clinical geneticist or genetic counselor.

Appendix: ACMG/AMP Evidence Code Reference

The following table describes the ACMG/AMP evidence codes used in this report for variant classification (Richards et al., Genet Med 2015; with ClinGen SVI updates 2018–2020).

Pathogenic Evidence

CodeStrengthDescription
PVS1Very StrongNull variant (nonsense, frameshift, canonical splice site, initiation codon, single/multi-exon deletion) in a gene where loss of function is a known mechanism of disease
PS1StrongSame amino acid change as a previously established pathogenic variant, regardless of nucleotide change
PS3StrongWell-established functional studies show a deleterious effect on the gene product
PS4StrongPrevalence of the variant in affected individuals is significantly increased compared to controls
PM1ModerateLocated in a mutational hotspot and/or critical, well-established functional domain
PM2ModerateAbsent or extremely low frequency in population databases (gnomAD, KOVA)
PM2_SupportingSupportingLow frequency in population databases — downgraded from Moderate per ClinGen SVI 2020
PP5SupportingReputable source recently reports variant as pathogenic (retained for legacy compatibility)

Benign Evidence

CodeStrengthDescription
BA1Stand-aloneAllele frequency >5% in any general continental population dataset — classified as Benign
BS1StrongAllele frequency greater than expected for the disorder (typically ≥1%)
BP1SupportingMissense variant in a gene for which primarily truncating variants are known to cause disease

Classification Criteria (ACMG/AMP 2015 Table 5)

ClassificationRequired Evidence Combinations
PathogenicPVS1 + ≥1 Strong (PS); OR 2 Strong (PS); OR 1 Strong + ≥3 Moderate
Likely PathogenicPVS1 + 1 Moderate (PM); OR 1 Strong + 1–2 Moderate; OR ≥3 Moderate
VUSCriteria for Benign or Pathogenic are not met, or evidence is contradictory
BenignBA1 (stand-alone); OR ≥2 Strong Benign (BS)
Drug ResponsePharmacogenomic variant — classified using CPIC guidelines, not ACMG Mendelian framework

Reference: Richards S, et al. Standards and guidelines for the interpretation of sequence variants. Genet Med. 2015;17(5):405-424. PMID: 25741868.

CPIC Evidence Levels for Pharmacogenomics (PGx)

LevelClassificationDescription
Level A Strong evidence Prescribing action recommended. Strong evidence with a published CPIC guideline providing specific dosing or drug selection recommendations.
Level B Moderate evidence Prescribing action may be recommended. Moderate evidence supports changing pharmacotherapy based on genetic information.
Level C Optional evidence Clinical action is optional. Evidence supports a gene-drug association, but the clinical impact is unclear.
Level D Informational only No prescribing action recommended. Evidence is insufficient to support changing clinical practice.

Reference: Relling MV, Klein TE. CPIC: Clinical Pharmacogenetics Implementation Consortium. Clin Pharmacol Ther. 2011;89(3):464-467. PMID: 21270786.
